Case Study

An Ongoing UI, label, manual Localization Program for an Innovative and Confidential Medical Device

  • Content Type: .JSON, FrameMaker, XML
  • Languages: 34 languages
  • Scope: ~40k word UI, label and ~500k word IFU localization
  • Stakeholders: Writers, technical communication managers, linguists, engineers, DTPers, QA analysts, program directors, sales, and etc.
  • My role: project manager managing the end-to-end ongoing program, ensuring on-budget, on-time delivery with uncompromising quality.
  • Tools used: SDL Trados Studio, MT, QA software, File Compare Tool, Muitl-Term Termbase, Notepad++, ChangeTracker, Project Management Platform.

Challenges and Solutions

Challenges from lack of information:

The client not providing information more than limited/zero context for UIs. No product images and no detailed descriptions. Requesting rush delivery

    Solutions
  • Conducting psedotranslation and machine translation to test the .JSON files.
  • Requesting linguists to finish translation ASAP without digging around
  • Guiding linguists (especially the ones with proven record of being responsible, responsive, and curious) to submit queries while moving the project along.
  • Creating and sending a well-organized master query sheet to the client meanwhile pushing translation files to engineering to ensure no time is wasted.
  • Having linguists implement changes based on the client answers prior to final QA. Performing PM checks for glaring areas and key terms before final delivery using a different QA software.

Challenges from large word count with rush timeline and frequent change requests:

30 IFUs/manuals

    Solutions
  • Cross checking high-risk sections to solve ambiguity, inconsistencies, and errors at early stage.
  • Applying MTPEQA to files with high percentage of repetitions and context matches.
  • Allocating multiple translators + one reviewer for each language.
  • Building preliminary translation memories for pretranslating updated files.
  • Performing centralized QA.
  • Iterating glossaries and global checklists.

Vendor Management

Low capacity and frequent delays due to holidays and frequent internal changes

    Solutions
  • Writing heads-up email to all affected local teams and their line managers.
  • Reviewing capacity heatmap, creating and maitaining local team dynamic report
  • Creating and providing straightforward project specific one-page instruction instead of using auto-generated instruction by the system for local teams.
  • Working with vendor relations to form backup teams.
